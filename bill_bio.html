Bill "Superfoot" Wallace

The Undefeated Kickboxing Champion

Bill "Superfoot" Wallace is one of the most legendary figures in martial arts history. Known for his incredible speed and undefeated professional kickboxing record (23-0, 13 KOs), Wallace dominated the sport in the 1970s as the PKA Middleweight Champion for nearly six years. His fighting style became iconic after a Judo injury forced him to rely almost entirely on his left leg, he developed devastating kicks that clocked an astonishing 60 mph. Beyond his competitive career, Wallace made significant contributions to martial arts as an educator, earning a master's degree in Kinesiology and authoring multiple instructional books. He also appeared in films alongside stars like Chuck Norris and Jackie Chan while training celebrities, including Elvis Presley.

Wallace's legacy extends far beyond his fighting career. As a respected teacher and innovator, he developed the "Superfoot System" and taught seminars worldwide, inspiring generations of martial artists. His authenticity and skill earned him roles as a UFC commentator and film actor, bridging the gap between competitive fighting and mainstream recognition. Tragically, Wallace was the personal trainer who discovered John Belushi after his fatal overdose, adding a somber note to his remarkable life story. Today, Wallace remains a celebrated figure in martial arts, a true champion whose influence continues through his teachings, writings, and unforgettable impact on the sport.
